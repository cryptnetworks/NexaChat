# Roadmap

## Bounded MVP

The MVP includes secure local accounts and sessions, community membership, categories and text spaces, a specified subset of roles and permissions, real-time text with edit/delete/reply, basic invitations, essential moderation, unread state, deployable storage, backups, and administrator health controls. Accessibility, retention behavior, auditability, and tested upgrades are release criteria.

It excludes voice/video, screen sharing, federation, peer-to-peer transport, public discovery, extension execution, full-text feature breadth, mobile-native clients, group calls, and parity with established platforms.

## Phases

- **Phase 0 — architecture and developer foundation:** contracts, domain boundaries, durable adapters and migrations, CI/security checks, Compose, observability baseline, authentication design, accessibility baseline, and deployment documentation.
- **Phase 1 — core collaboration:** accounts, secure sessions, communities, membership, text spaces, messages, invitations, and the tested permission evaluator.
- **Phase 2 — safety and reach:** moderation cases, reports and appeals, direct conversations, search, synchronized notifications, retention, export, and administration controls.
- **Phase 3 — voice:** SFU selection and deployment, signaling, permission-controlled voice rooms, device controls, diagnostics, and capacity testing.
- **Phase 4 — richer media and ecosystem:** video, screen sharing, scoped extensions, webhooks, desktop hardening, backup/update maturity, and release artifacts.
- **Phase 5 — federation or peer-to-peer research:** threat model, identity and trust design, moderation and abuse model, metadata privacy, NAT/offline delivery, and consistency prototypes. Implementation requires a separate accepted decision.
